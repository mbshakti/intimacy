Enneagram of Personality

A model of the psyche with nine interconnected personality types, each defined by a core fear and core desire. Each type has a characteristic role and way of engaging with the world.

The Nine Types

1The Reformer GutPrincipled, purposeful, self-controlled, perfectionistic. Core fear: being corrupt or evil.
2The Helper HeartCaring, interpersonal, demonstrative, possessive. Core fear: being unloved.
3The Achiever HeartAdaptable, excelling, driven, image-conscious. Core fear: being worthless.
4The Individualist HeartExpressive, dramatic, self-absorbed, temperamental. Core fear: having no identity or significance.
5The Investigator HeadPerceptive, innovative, secretive, isolated. Core fear: being helpless or incapable.
6The Loyalist HeadEngaging, responsible, anxious, suspicious. Core fear: being without support or guidance.
7The Enthusiast HeadSpontaneous, versatile, acquisitive, scattered. Core fear: being trapped in pain or deprivation.
8The Challenger GutSelf-confident, decisive, willful, confrontational. Core fear: being controlled or harmed by others.
9The Peacemaker GutReceptive, reassuring, complacent, resigned. Core fear: loss and separation.

Wings

Each type is influenced by one of its two adjacent types, called the wing. A type 4 with a 3-wing (4w3) is more ambitious and image-conscious than a 4w5, which is more withdrawn and cerebral.

Centers of Intelligence

Gut (8, 9, 1) — instinct, body-based, anger. Heart (2, 3, 4) — emotion, image, shame. Head (5, 6, 7) — thought, fear, planning.
