Bots seem like a new type of hypermodern form of content.

Roleplay bots function at the level of entertainment: you're a character in a story, the bot is playing a knight or a villain or a morally complicated stranger. The frame is fiction, closer to a game than to a relationship.

Companion apps operate at the level of intimacy (or the simulation of it). I spoke to the founder of an AI girlfriend app who told me, "do you know what sex work is? at its core, it's the illusion of intimacy."

LLM bots, at least right now, have very poor memory. Continuity is what makes the illusion of intimacy possible. At present, the user projects the entire relationship.
