In this world, “personality” does not mean inner life or the Jungian self rooted in lived experience, conflict, and transformation. 

Rather, in this world, personality is a collection of hyperspecific prompts that attempt to instruct the bot on how to engage with a user.  

It looks like a JSON file.

No single field defines the character. In the language model, personality emerges from how all these fields interact with everything else. 


At its simplest, JSON is a way of organizing information. Labels on one side, details on the other. Inside it lives: the bot’s fears, likes, dislikes, example dialogue, the bot's height, weight, physical attributes. 


Cyberpunk writers have explored the great dream of the cyberspace as the ultimate chance to finally make an escape from the human body. A place for final autonomy, hypercontrol, hyperspecificity. A place where the soul is finally reignited with itself. 

It's funny, then, reading the JSONs to learn that characters are very much rooted in a "body" - although that body sometimes is half-animal, half-human, or fully animal, or an entirely new creature — wolf and bat and spider fused into one creature that has opinions about its own femininity. The body doesn't seem forgotten here, just reinvented.
